## What Is Supply and Demand in Trading?

Supply and demand represent the **core force behind price movement**.

- **Supply** = Sellers willing to sell at certain prices
    
- **Demand** = Buyers willing to buy at certain prices
    

### How Price Reacts:

- Strong demand ‚Üí Price rises
    
- Strong supply ‚Üí Price falls
    
- Weak supply & demand ‚Üí Price moves sideways
    
- Strong supply & demand together ‚Üí Range-bound market
    

Every market‚ÄîForex, crypto, stocks, commodities‚Äîmoves because of this interaction.

## Supply and Demand Zones Explained

Instead of focusing on single price levels, professional traders use **zones**.

### Demand Zone:

- Area where price dropped strongly, then reversed upward aggressively
    
- Indicates strong buying interest
    

### Supply Zone:

- Area where price rose, then dropped sharply
    
- Indicates strong selling pressure
    

üí° **Key Insight:**  
Supply and demand zones are created by **institutional orders**, not retail traders.

## Why Institutions Move Price in Stages

Institutions trade **huge volumes**, so they:

- Cannot enter trades at once
    
- Need liquidity to fill orders
    
- Break orders into multiple parts
    

This causes:

- Fake breakouts
    
- Pullbacks
    
- Liquidity grabs
    
- Sudden strong moves
    

Understanding this behavior is the foundation of SMC.

## How to Draw Supply and Demand Zones Correctly

### Step 1: Look for Impulsive Moves

Strong, fast price movements indicate institutional activity.

### Step 2: Identify the Origin

The candle (or candles) **before the impulse** create the zone.

### Step 3: Mark the Zone

- Demand zone: From candle body to lowest wick
    
- Supply zone: From candle body to highest wick
    

üîë **Best zones** cause:

- Strong impulsive movement
    
- Break of previous high or low

## Market Structure Explained

**Market structure** describes how price forms:

- Higher highs & higher lows (uptrend)
    
- Lower highs & lower lows (downtrend)
    
- Sideways movement (range)
    

Every movement between a high and a low forms a **structure**.

---

## Break of Structure (BOS)

**Break of Structure (BOS)** happens when price:

- Breaks a previous high in an uptrend
    
- Breaks a previous low in a downtrend
    
- Closes decisively beyond that level
    

BOS confirms **trend continuation**.

üö´ Wicks alone are NOT enough ‚Äî candle close matters.

---

## Change of Character (CHOCH)

**Change of Character (CHOCH)** signals a **trend reversal**.

Example:

- Market makes higher highs
    
- Suddenly breaks the last valid low
    
- Trend shifts from bullish to bearish
    

CHOCH is often the **first sign of institutional trend change**.

---

## Liquidity: The Fuel of the Market

Liquidity exists:

- Above highs (buy stops)
    
- Below lows (sell stops)
    
- At equal highs & equal lows
    

Institutions **hunt liquidity** to:

- Fill large orders
    
- Trigger retail stop losses
    
- Create strong price movements
    

---

## Liquidity Grab vs Break of Structure

|Liquidity Grab|Break of Structure|
|---|---|
|Quick spike|Strong impulsive move|
|No strong close|Clear candle close|
|Fake breakout|Trend confirmation|
|Returns inside range|Continues direction|

Understanding this difference protects you from manipulation.
